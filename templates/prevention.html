Take steps to protect yourself

Stay At Home

Staying at home is one of the most effective ways to prevent the spread of COVID-19. It reduces the risk of exposure to the virus and limits its transmission within the community.

By avoiding crowded places and unnecessary outings, you protect yourself and others, especially the elderly and those with weak immunity. Staying home also supports healthcare systems by slowing down the infection rate and giving medical professionals time to respond effectively.

Cover cough & sneezes

To prevent the spread of COVID-19, always cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when you cough or sneeze. This helps stop respiratory droplets from reaching others. Dispose of used tissues immediately and wash your hands with soap or use hand sanitizer. Avoid coughing into your hands, as it can transfer the virus to surfaces and people around you.

Wear a face mask

Wearing a face mask is one of the most effective ways to prevent the spread of COVID-19. Masks help block respiratory droplets that may carry the virus, especially in crowded or indoor settings. They protect both the wearer and those around them. It’s important to wear a mask properly—covering both nose and mouth—and to avoid touching it while wearing. Use clean, reusable or disposable masks daily.

Clean & disinfectant

Regularly cleaning and disinfecting frequently touched surfaces is essential to prevent the spread of COVID-19. Items such as doorknobs, light switches, mobile phones, and countertops can carry the virus. Use alcohol-based disinfectants or diluted bleach solutions for effective cleaning. Wash your hands before and after cleaning. Maintaining hygiene at home and workspaces helps reduce the risk of transmission and keeps everyone safe, especially in shared or high-traffic areas.

Maintain Social Distancing

Keeping a safe distance of at least 6 feet (2 meters) from others, especially in public or crowded spaces, helps reduce the chances of inhaling droplets from coughs, sneezes, or conversations. Social distancing is especially important around people not from your household or those who may be unvaccinated. Avoid large gatherings and choose outdoor spaces over indoor settings to reduce your risk of exposure to the virus.

Get Vaccinated and Stay Updated

Vaccination is a key defense against COVID-19. Vaccines reduce the risk of severe illness, hospitalization, and death. Ensure you receive the recommended number of doses and booster shots as advised by health authorities. Vaccines help build community immunity and slow the spread of variants. Stay informed about new updates and consult health professionals for vaccine availability and eligibility in your area.
