What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.

Symptoms include fever, cough, fatigue, and shortness of breath. Severe cases can lead to pneumonia and death, especially in older adults and those with existing health conditions. Preventive measures include wearing masks, handwashing, physical distancing, and vaccination to reduce transmission and protect public health.

How does COVID-19 Spread?

COVID-19 spreads mainly through respiratory droplets and contact with contaminated surfaces. It can also be transmitted through close contact with infected individuals or animals.

  1. Respiratory Droplets Transmission:

    COVID-19 mainly spreads through droplets released when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of nearby people or be inhaled, leading to possible infection.

  2. Surface (Fomite) Transmission:

    The virus can survive on surfaces like door handles, phones, and tables. When someone touches these objects and then their face, the virus may enter the body through the mouth, nose, or eyes.

  3. Close and Asymptomatic Contact:

    Close contact with infected individuals, even without visible symptoms, increases transmission risk. Brief interactions without masks or distancing can easily spread the virus in crowded or poorly ventilated places.

Stay at home

Staying at home minimizes your exposure to the virus, especially during outbreaks. Avoiding crowded places and travel reduces the risk of infection and helps prevent community spread.

Cover cough & sneezes

Always cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when coughing or sneezing to stop the spread of infectious droplets and protect others in shared environments.

Wear a facemask

Wearing a mask in public acts as a protective barrier against airborne droplets, reducing your risk and protecting those around you in crowded or enclosed spaces.

Clean & disinfectant

Clean high-touch surfaces regularly like doorknobs, phones, and counters. Disinfecting helps stop the virus from spreading indirectly via hands or objects.
