Recognize COVID-19 Symptoms

Early detection helps prevent the spread.

Fever

One of the most common symptoms.Fever is a temporary rise in body temperature, often due to infection. It’s the body’s natural response to fight off viruses or bacteria. Common symptoms include sweating, chills, headache, and weakness. While usually not dangerous, persistent high fever may indicate a serious condition and should be evaluated by a healthcare provider.

Dry Cough

Persistent cough without mucus.Cough is a common COVID-19 symptom, often dry and persistent. It may start mildly but can worsen over time, irritating the throat and chest. Unlike a productive cough, COVID-related cough usually lacks mucus. If accompanied by fever, fatigue, or breathing issues, it may indicate infection and should be monitored closely.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing may be a sign of severe infection.Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing is a serious symptom of COVID-19, indicating possible lung involvement or low oxygen levels. It may start mildly but worsen quickly. This symptom requires immediate medical attention, especially if it’s sudden or severe, as it can lead to complications like pneumonia or respiratory failure.

Nausea or vomiting

Nausea or vomiting can be symptoms of COVID-19, especially in early stages or in children. These digestive issues may occur alongside other signs like fever or fatigue. Though not as common as respiratory symptoms, they can signal infection. Persistent or severe symptoms should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.

Fatigue

Fatigue is a common symptom of COVID-19, marked by extreme tiredness and lack of energy. It can persist even after mild infection, affecting daily activities. Fatigue may be physical or mental and is often accompanied by muscle weakness, low motivation, or poor concentration. Rest and hydration can help manage it.

Congestion or Runny nose

Congestion or a runny nose is a mild but common COVID-19 symptom, especially with newer variants. It occurs when nasal tissues become inflamed, producing excess mucus. This can cause stuffiness, sneezing, or postnasal drip. Though similar to a cold or allergies, it should be monitored, especially if other symptoms develop.
